# 3.9 Accuracy of Position Moves

## Objective

Quantify the positioning accuracy and repeatability of the linear stage at a representative mid-stroke location, and compare against the encoder-reported position and the manufacturer's specification.

## Equipment


| Item                              | Specification                                                            |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Stage                             | THK KR3306A-0500, 6 mm lead, 500 mm stroke                               |
| Motor                             | Kollmorgen AKM21G-ENMN2-00                                               |
| Encoder                           | 2048 PPR incremental, 8192 cnts/rev (quadrature)                         |
| Theoretical resolution            | 6 mm / 8192 = 0.7324 μm/count (1365.33 cnts/mm)                          |
| Controller                        | Galil DMC, axis C                                                        |
| Reference instrument              | Dial test indicator (DTI), ±200 μm range, 1 μm graduation, magnetic base |
| Long-range reference              | Calipers (for lead verification only)                                    |
| Manufacturer spec (repeatability) | ±10 μm                                                                   |
| Manufacturer spec (backlash)      | 0.02 mm (≈27 cnts)                                                       |


## Test Conditions


| Parameter                    | Value                                                          |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Test location                | 200 mm from home (273066 cnts)                                 |
| Servo gains                  | KP = 20, KD = 100, KI = 0.05                                   |
| Test speed                   | SPC = 5000 cnts/s (≈3.66 mm/s)                                 |
| Test accel/decel             | ACC = DCC = 35000 cnts/s²                                      |
| DTI mounting                 | Plunger contact on +X face of carriage, preloaded to mid-range |
| Settling time before reading | 2000 ms                                                        |


## Method

Implemented in `demo_accuracy_C.dmc`.

1. Home against the −X limit and back off 200 cnts; redefine `DPC = 0`.
2. Move to the test location at 200 mm and pause for DTI mounting.
3. Backlash takeup: 3 cycles of `PRC = ±1000` to seat the ball nut against the +X side of its dead band.
4. Redefine `DPC = 0` at the takeup endpoint and zero the DTI to mid-range.
5. Run 2 unrecorded warm-up cycles (mechanical/lubrication settling).
6. Run 5 measurement cycles. Each cycle visits three targets, all approached from the −X side (the screw nut is loaded against the same side of its dead band before every reading):
  - **A1:** 50 μm (68 cnts)
  - **A2:** 100 μm (137 cnts)
  - **A3:** 150 μm (205 cnts)
7. At each target, wait 2 s for servo settling, then record `_TPC` (encoder) and the DTI.

Targets were sized to fit within the DTI's ±200 μm range with margin for the −100 cnt approach overshoot.

## Results

### Raw Data


| Cycle | A1 `_TPC` | A1 DTI (μm) | A2 `_TPC` | A2 DTI (μm) | A3 `_TPC` | A3 DTI (μm) |
| ----- | --------- | ----------- | --------- | ----------- | --------- | ----------- |
| 0     | 68        | 49          | 137       | 99          | 205       | 149         |
| 1     | 68        | 49          | 137       | 99          | 205       | 149         |
| 2     | 68        | 49          | 137       | 99          | 205       | 149         |
| 3     | 68        | 49          | 137       | 99          | 205       | 149         |
| 4     | 68        | 49          | 137       | 99          | 205       | 149         |


### Servo (Encoder) Performance

`_TPC` = commanded counts on every cycle for every target. Servo following error at rest was **0 counts** in all 15 measurements.

### Mechanical (DTI) Performance


| Target | Cmd cnts | Encoder-predicted DTI (μm) | Measured DTI (μm) | Δ (μm) |
| ------ | -------- | -------------------------- | ----------------- | ------ |
| A1     | 68       | 49.8                       | 49                | −0.8   |
| A2     | 137      | 100.4                      | 99                | −1.4   |
| A3     | 205      | 150.2                      | 149               | −1.2   |


Mean offset = −1.1 μm (DTI under-reads encoder-predicted position).

### Repeatability

Across 5 cycles, DTI readings were identical to within the instrument's 1 μm resolution at all three targets (standard deviation = 0).

### Incremental (Point-to-Point) Accuracy


| Increment | Commanded Δ (μm) | Measured Δ (DTI, μm) | Error (μm) |
| --------- | ---------------- | -------------------- | ---------- |
| A1 → A2   | 50.0             | 50.0                 | 0          |
| A2 → A3   | 50.0             | 50.0                 | 0          |


## Discussion

The stage exhibits effectively perfect closed-loop behavior at the encoder level. With the integral gain raised to KI = 0.05, the servo holds zero following error against the DTI's spring force, which had previously been masking true positioning performance. Without integral action the DTI preload pulled the carriage off-target by several counts; with the tuned PID the steady-state error vanishes within the 2 s settling window.

DTI readings track the encoder-predicted positions with a small (≈1 μm) systematic shortfall that is constant across all three targets. This is consistent with either a sub-micron DTI zero offset at takeup or a small probe cosine error from non-perfect perpendicularity of the plunger to the motion axis. It is *not* a stage error, because (a) the offset does not grow with travel distance, (b) the increment-to-increment moves are exact (50 μm commanded → 50 μm measured at both A1→A2 and A2→A3), and (c) the encoder confirms the carriage reaches the commanded count.

Repeatability at this position is below the DTI's 1 μm graduation: every cycle returned the same reading. The actual sub-micron repeatability cannot be resolved with this instrument but is bounded above at < 1 μm, well below the manufacturer's ±10 μm specification. The unidirectional approach pattern (`PAC = target − 100; PAC = target`) and the 3-cycle backlash takeup were essential for this result — they ensure each measurement loads the screw nut on the same side of its 0.02 mm dead band, eliminating backlash from the unidirectional accuracy figure.

The two warm-up cycles were also important. Cycle 0 of the very first run differed slightly from later cycles before warm-up was added; with warm-up included, every measurement cycle returns identical data.

## Conclusion


| Metric                               | Result                                           | Mfr. spec      | Pass |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------ | -------------- | ---- |
| Unidirectional repeatability         | < 1 μm (below DTI floor)                         | ±10 μm         | ✓    |
| Point-to-point accuracy (50 μm step) | 0 μm error                                       | n/a            | ✓    |
| Absolute accuracy from DTI zero      | ≈ 1 μm constant offset (instrument-attributable) | ±10 μm typical | ✓    |
| Servo following error at rest        | 0 counts                                         | n/a            | ✓    |


The stage and servo perform approximately an order of magnitude better than the manufacturer's ±10 μm repeatability specification at this operating point. Residual disagreement between encoder and DTI is consistent with measurement-instrument artifacts rather than stage performance.

# 3.10 Actual vs Theoretical Resolution

## Objective

Determine the smallest commanded motion that produces a real, observable mechanical displacement of the carriage, and compare against the theoretical encoder-limited resolution.

## Equipment

Same as Test 3.9 (THK KR3306A-0500 stage, AKM21G motor, 8192 cnt/rev encoder, ±200 μm DTI).

## Theoretical Resolution


\text{Resolution} = \frac{\text{lead}}{\text{counts/rev}} = \frac{6\text{mm}}{8192} = 0.7324\ \mu\text{m/count}


This is the smallest move the controller can command. Whether each commanded count produces real carriage motion is governed by:

- Encoder quantization (1 count = 0.7324 μm)
- DTI graduation (1 μm)
- Stiction / Coulomb friction in the ball nut and rail bearings (manufacturer starting torque spec: 0.07 N·m)

## Test Conditions

Same test location, gains, and DTI mounting as Test 3.9. ACC = DCC = 10000 cnts/s² for the resolution test (gentler trapezoid for sub-micron commands).

## Method

Implemented in `demo_resolution_C.dmc`.

1. Home, move to 200 mm test position.
2. Mount and preload DTI to mid-range.
3. Backlash takeup: 3× ±1000 cnt; redefine `DPC = 0`; zero DTI.
4. Run a sequence of incremental + moves and record `_TPC`, `_TEC`, and DTI at each:
  - Ten consecutive 1-count steps
  - Single 5-count step
  - Single 14-count step
  - Single 25-count step
  - Single 50-count step
  - Single 100-count step

Step sizes are chosen to span from the encoder's smallest increment up to a fraction of the DTI's full-scale range, allowing both sub-micron and supra-micron behavior to be characterized in a single sequence.

## Results

### Encoder

For all ten 1-count steps: `_TEC = 0`, `_TPC` advanced by exactly +1 each step. The closed loop responds to and lands on every individual encoder count.

For the larger steps:


| Step       | Cmd cumulative cnts | `_TPC` | Δ from prev `_TPC` | Comment                  |
| ---------- | ------------------- | ------ | ------------------ | ------------------------ |
| 1 cnt × 10 | 10                  | 10     | +1 each            | Exact                    |
| +5 cnt     | 15                  | 16     | +6                 | 1-cnt overshoot, settled |
| +14 cnt    | 29                  | 29     | +13                | Self-corrected           |
| +25 cnt    | 54                  | 54     | +25                | Exact                    |
| +50 cnt    | 104                 | 105    | +51                | 1-cnt overshoot, settled |
| +100 cnt   | 204                 | 204    | +99                | Self-corrected           |


Two single-count overshoots within the recorded set; both self-corrected before the next move. Mean encoder error ≈ 0 counts.

### DTI


| Step                          | `_TPC` | Encoder-predicted DTI (μm) = `_TPC` × 0.7324 | Measured DTI (μm) | Δ DTI vs prev (μm) | Cmd Δ (μm) |
| ----------------------------- | ------ | -------------------------------------------- | ----------------- | ------------------ | ---------- |
| After step 9 (cum 1-cnt × 10) | 10     | 7.32                                         | 6.5               | 6.5                | 7.32       |
| 5-cnt                         | 16     | 11.72                                        | 10.0              | 3.5                | 3.66       |
| 14-cnt                        | 29     | 21.24                                        | 20.0              | 10.0               | 10.25      |
| 25-cnt                        | 54     | 39.55                                        | 38.5              | 18.5               | 18.31      |
| 50-cnt                        | 105    | 76.90                                        | 75.5              | 37.0               | 36.62      |
| 100-cnt                       | 204    | 149.41                                       | 147.0             | 71.5               | 73.24      |


### 1-Count Trajectory Behavior

During the ten consecutive 1-count steps, the DTI moved in response to every commanded step, but the trajectory of each individual ≈0.73 μm move was not smooth: small visible jumps occurred during the trapezoidal velocity profile. The motion was neither continuously sliding nor stuck-then-released in chunks (which would have caused some commands to be missed at the DTI). Every command produced a deterministic settled endpoint, and the cumulative deflection of 6.5 μm across the ten steps is consistent with the encoder-predicted 7.32 μm.

### Linearity

Mean measured displacement per commanded count, by step size:


| Step size (cnts)   | Cmd Δ (μm) | Measured Δ (μm) | μm/count |
| ------------------ | ---------- | --------------- | -------- |
| 1 (cumulative ×10) | 7.32       | 6.5             | 0.65     |
| 5                  | 3.66       | 3.5             | 0.70     |
| 14                 | 10.25      | 10.0            | 0.71     |
| 25                 | 18.31      | 18.5            | 0.74     |
| 50                 | 36.62      | 37.0            | 0.74     |
| 100                | 73.24      | 71.5            | 0.72     |


Mean ≈ **0.71 μm/count** measured vs **0.7324 μm/count** theoretical → 3.0% under-read.

## Discussion

At the encoder level the system achieves its theoretical resolution exactly. Servo following error remained zero on every single-count command and the controller corrected the two intermediate overshoots without losing position. There is no encoder-level lost motion at the 1-count scale.

At the mechanical (DTI) level the carriage also responds to every 1-count command, but the *manner* of motion at this scale is different from larger moves. Each 0.73 μm trapezoid contained micro stick-slip — small visible jumps within an otherwise trapezoidal velocity profile. This is the expected friction regime: the breakaway force in the ball nut and recirculating ball rails is comparable in magnitude to the force required for a 0.73 μm advance, so the carriage moves in a series of tiny discrete slips rather than a continuous slide. Crucially, this stick-slip did not cost commands: the cumulative DTI displacement after ten 1-count steps (6.5 μm) is within reading tolerance of the theoretical 7.32 μm, indicating no commands were absorbed into a quasi-static dead band.

This means the practical resolution depends on the application:

- **For static point-to-point positioning,** the practical resolution equals the theoretical 0.73 μm. Each command produces a deterministic settled position.
- **For smooth, continuous sub-micron-velocity motion,** practical resolution is friction-limited. The stage will advance in micro-slips and cannot glide at this scale; this is a fundamental property of mechanical preloaded stages, not a defect of this unit.

The DTI readings systematically read ≈3% low across all step sizes. Because the offset is multiplicative on displacement (not constant), it is dominantly a probe cosine error: a probe angled by θ off-perpendicular reads cos(θ) of the true displacement, and a 3% shortfall corresponds to θ ≈ 14°. A small zero/calibration component cannot be ruled out but is bounded by the absolute offset seen in 3.9 (≈1 μm constant). The encoder is the trusted reference at this scale; the DTI is sufficient to confirm motion exists at every commanded step.

## Conclusion


| Metric                                  | Result                                          |
| --------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Theoretical resolution                  | 0.7324 μm/count                                 |
| Servo (encoder) resolution              | 1 count, `_TEC = 0` on every step               |
| Mechanical resolution at DTI (endpoint) | 1 count = 0.73 μm, deterministic                |
| Trajectory smoothness at 0.73 μm        | Micro stick-slip within each move               |
| Practical positioning resolution        | **0.73 μm = theoretical**                       |
| Practical smooth-motion resolution      | Friction-limited; not single-count              |
| Measured linearity                      | 0.71 μm/count avg (≈3% short, DTI cosine error) |
| Cumulative deflection over 10× 1-cnt    | 6.5 μm (theoretical 7.32 μm)                    |


The stage achieves its theoretical encoder-limited resolution for endpoint positioning. No commanded counts are lost.
